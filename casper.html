Casper*

A checkout redesign with measurable lift across attachment rates and revenue.

*Casper is a direct-to-consumer sleep brand selling mattresses, pillows, and bedding online and across 932+ retail locations in the US. At the time of this project, Casper was in a high-growth Series B phase with 1.5+ million customers and a focus on e-commerce conversion optimization.

PRODUCT

A cross-sell redesign within Casper's e-commerce checkout, tested against 466,000 users during the company's Series B scaling phase. The goal: lift attachment rates at peak purchase intent without disrupting checkout conversion.

ROLE

Product Designer, working alongside a UX strategist, with ownership of the visual and interaction design.

PROBLEM

The cart cross-sell carousel was underperforming. Auto-rotation, low visual contrast, and unclear mobile interactions were generating poor attachment rates at the highest-intent moment in the purchase journey. A poorly designed cross-sell can also increase checkout drop-off, so any redesign had to lift attachment without touching conversion.

STAGE

Series B scaling phase, 1.5+ million customers.

STRATEGY

The initiative was built around a clear constraint: minimal engineering lift, maximum measurable signal. The cart cross-sell was identified as the right target: peak purchase intent, isolated for clean A/B testing, and a direct revenue metric. I was brought in to design within that framework.

PHILOSOPHY

Low engineering investment. Fast iteration. Measurable impact.

Cross-sell redesign iterations

DESIGN

Three problems in the existing carousel: it moved too fast to engage, dot navigation was unintuitive on mobile, and card design lacked contrast at a high-cognitive-load moment. I explored visual treatments, CTA hierarchy, contextual copy, and interaction patterns.

DECISIONS

Final changes: slowed carousel movement, arrow navigation replacing dots, stronger card design, and contextual copy tailored to each product. Every decision isolated a variable for clean A/B signal.

OUTCOMES

Attachment rates improved across every product tested, with gains ranging from 7% to 20%. Revenue per user lifted 1%, projecting to $500K+ annually.

VALIDATION

Primary checkout conversion held unchanged. The redesign lifted cross-sell without adding friction. Design is a growth lever.
