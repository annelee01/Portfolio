Verizon, AKQA*

Designing for one of the largest consumer product experiences in the US.

*AKQA is a leading global design and innovation agency that creates digital products, services, and brand experiences to make a positive difference in people's lives. Part of WPP, it specializes in combining technology, creativity, and data to help brands, including Nike and Visa, connect with customers through, for example, interactive campaigns, mobile apps, and, spatial design. Recognized as a premier, highly awarded agency for digital innovation.

PRODUCT

The My Verizon App is a self-service platform for 145+ million subscribers, one of the largest consumer product experiences in the US. My work was focused on the Prepaid segment, a distinct subset of the business with its own pricing models, business rules, and account management needs.

ROLE

Senior Designer, AKQA.

CHALLENGE

Two constraints made this project unusually complex. Prepaid was a newer segment with actively evolving business rules, requiring design to absorb frequent changes without disrupting the experience. Simultaneously, every production screen was designed against a new brand system still being implemented.

SCOPE

UI design and design system work across shop, plan management, and profile flows. Cross-functional collaboration with Verizon product, UX, and engineering stakeholders.

DESIGN SYSTEM

The app runs on an extensive component library of 120 templates and 850 modules. I reorganized its structure to enable collaborative, parallel prototyping across the team, delivered pixel-perfect production-ready screens, and advocated for new components only when new business use cases required them.

IMPACT

Shipped production-ready screens across plan management, profile, and shop flows. The Shop feature launched unannounced and self-discovered within the main menu, with 750 phones purchased in the first two months and no dedicated marketing push.
