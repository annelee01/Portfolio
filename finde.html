Finde*

Building a marketplace from zero to launch.

*Finde is a vintage fashion marketplace built to make secondhand discovery feel curated and intentional. Designed and engineered independently on Django, Python, and AWS, it aggregates structured inventory with a cross-category filtering system built for real-time, in-session search. The goal was to replicate the serendipity of in-store vintage shopping at the scale of an online marketplace.

PRODUCT

A vintage fashion marketplace designed around how secondhand discovery actually works. Structured listings, real-time cross-category filtering, and an experience shaped by how buying intent forms.

ROLE

Founder, Product Designer & Builder

PROBLEM

Secondhand marketplaces are fragmented by default: categories are unreliable, inventory quality varies wildly, and trust breaks down before purchase intent can form. Discovery is not just a browsing problem. It is a trust problem, and trust at scale is a growth problem.

SCOPE

End-to-end build from concept to launch: brand identity, UI/UX design, and full-stack engineering.

THE BET

Prove that discovery drives engagement. Then use that demand signal to pull sellers in organically.

STRATEGY

I bootstrapped supply through eBay's developer API, launching Finde with real structured inventory from day one. That gave me a live demand signal to validate buyer intent before attractong sellers and building out the supply side.

BRAND

Finde needed to feel curated, not utilitarian. The identity system was built around warmth and restraint — a serif wordmark, a muted earth palette, and a custom icon set that prioritised clarity at small sizes.

SYSTEM

Typography, color, iconography, UI and motion were designed together from the start, so the brand scaled consistently across all touchpoints.

DESIGN

Early user testing proved that emotional browsing happens before item filtering, so experience was designed discovery-first. Curated toggles like 'Date Night' were built around how buying decisions form.

FILTER SYSTEM

Most marketplaces search within a category. Finde searches across all of them. Enter your size and keyword and see every matching garment type. Tops, bottoms, shoes — returned together in one tailored view, built around outfits rather than isolated items.

PROCESS

Each iteration of the filter system design was pressure-tested against competitive references and specific usability criteria. The final design: horizontal dynamic tabs for fast context-switching across the full viewport.

ARCHITECTURE

Search results update instantly, without page reloads. The backend was built around that requirement before a single screen was designed.

IMPACT

With an initial beta release, results were more qualitative than quantitative. Scale was not the immediate objective. Organic users reached 28 countries, and session data confirmed high-intent behavior: successful filter and search interactions.

WHAT'S NEXT

The next phase deepens engagement through editorialized lifestyle content, while direct seller outreach informs onboarding tools to grow the supply side. Invite-only curation keeps quality high as the marketplace scales.
